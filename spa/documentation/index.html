Key ideas

          
    
            
  • Through keeps most power on the feeder; drop peels a smaller fraction to the premise.
    • 
            
  • Asymmetry is intentional — unlike a balanced 1:N hut split.
    • 
            
  • Insertion loss (dB) accumulates on every span, connector, and the tap itself.
    • 
            
  • Rx window — design so ONT light lands between min and max with margin.
    • 
            
  • Upstream — ONT bursts rejoin the feeder and are recovered at the OLT (optical tree + MAC scheduling).
    • 
            
  • Rural — long linear routes and sparse homes favor cascaded taps over wasting budget on dense star splits.
